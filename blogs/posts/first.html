What I'm Currently Reading — and Why It's Changing How I Think?

I'm currently a fan of The Uncommon Sense of Marketing by Sanjay Tiwari, and its core message is one every marketer needs to hear: “Get the basics right – and everything else follows”

But the insights go far deeper than “basics.” Tiwari unpacks one of the most important distinctions in all of marketing: the difference between a need and a want.

That gap between need and want is where all great marketing lives. The brands that win aren't just visible — they're inevitable. They've already positioned themselves so precisely in the consumer's mind that when the need appears, their name surfaces automatically. Tiwari breaks down exactly how companies build those brand personas, create emotional associations, and earn that mental real estate long before the consumer reaches for their wallet.

For me, this isn’t just theory. It’s a lens I now apply to every campaign I run, every piece of content I plan, and every brand I study. Reading this has changed how I look at every ad I see, every brand I work with, and every strategy I build.
